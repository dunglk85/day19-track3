OpenAI is an American artificial intelligence (AI) research organization founded in December 2015. 
The organization was founded by Sam Altman, Elon Musk, Ilya Sutskever, Greg Brockman, Trevor Blackwell, Vicki Cheung, Andrej Karpathy, Durk Kingma, Jessica Livingston, John Schulman, Pamela Vagata, and Wojciech Zaremba.
Sam Altman currently serves as the CEO of OpenAI.
OpenAI's mission is to ensure that artificial general intelligence (AGI) benefits all of humanity. 
One of its most famous products is ChatGPT, a conversational AI model launched in November 2022. 
OpenAI has a strong partnership with Microsoft, which has invested billions of dollars into the organization to power its Azure AI infrastructure.
The company transitioned from a non-profit to a "capped-profit" model in 2019 to attract more capital for its compute-intensive research.
OpenAI's headquarters are located in San Francisco, California.
In 2024, OpenAI released GPT-4o, a multimodal model capable of processing text, audio, and images in real-time.
Other notable products include DALL-E for image generation and Sora for video generation.
Ilya Sutskever, one of the co-founders and Chief Scientist, left the company in May 2024 to start a new venture called Safe Superintelligence (SSI).
OpenAI remains at the forefront of the AI race, competing directly with companies like Google and Anthropic.
