Google LLC is an American multinational technology company that specializes in Internet-related services and products.
It was founded on September 4, 1998, by Larry Page and Sergey Brin while they were PhD students at Stanford University in California.
Sundar Pichai is the current CEO of Google and its parent company, Alphabet Inc.
Google's mission is "to organize the world's information and make it universally accessible and useful."
Google is a leader in AI research through its Google DeepMind division, formed by merging Google Brain and DeepMind in 2023.
Demis Hassabis serves as the CEO of Google DeepMind.
Google's flagship AI model is Gemini, which competes directly with OpenAI's GPT-4.
Gemini is integrated into various Google products, including Search, Gmail, and Google Cloud.
The company's headquarters, known as the Googleplex, are located in Mountain View, California.
Google Cloud Platform (GCP) provides infrastructure and tools for developers to build AI applications, competing with Amazon Web Services (AWS) and Microsoft Azure.
In May 2024, at Google I/O, the company announced "AI Overviews" in Search, using generative AI to summarize complex topics.
Google also develops the Android mobile operating system, the Chrome web browser, and YouTube, the world's largest video-sharing platform.
Jeff Dean is a prominent computer scientist and Google Senior Fellow, known for his work on Google's core infrastructure and deep learning.
Google has made significant investments in hardware, such as the Tensor Processing Unit (TPU), specifically designed for machine learning workloads.
