Microsoft Corporation is an American multinational technology corporation headquartered in Redmond, Washington.
It was founded by Bill Gates and Paul Allen on April 4, 1975.
Satya Nadella is the current CEO of Microsoft, having taken over from Steve Ballmer in 2014.
Microsoft is one of the world's most valuable companies, known for the Windows operating system, Office productivity suite, and Azure cloud computing platform.
Under Nadella's leadership, Microsoft has shifted its focus heavily towards AI and cloud computing.
Microsoft has a strategic partnership with OpenAI, providing the organization with massive compute power on Azure in exchange for early access to its models.
Microsoft's AI assistant, Copilot, is powered by OpenAI's GPT models and is integrated across Windows 11, Microsoft 365, and GitHub.
Azure AI Services allow businesses to build their own AI applications using pre-trained models from Microsoft and OpenAI.
In 2024, Microsoft announced "Copilot+ PCs," a new category of Windows PCs designed for AI with dedicated Neural Processing Units (NPUs).
Kevin Scott serves as Microsoft's Chief Technology Officer (CTO) and has been instrumental in the OpenAI partnership.
Microsoft also owns GitHub, LinkedIn, and the Xbox gaming brand.
The company is competing with Google and Amazon to dominate the cloud AI market.
Mustafa Suleyman, a co-founder of DeepMind, joined Microsoft in 2024 as the CEO of Microsoft AI, a new division focused on consumer AI products.
Microsoft is also developing its own custom AI chips, such as the Azure Maia AI Accelerator, to reduce dependency on NVIDIA.
